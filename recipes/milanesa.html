Description

The Milanesa has its origins in Milan, Italy. ispired by the austrian schnitzel,the milanese made their own version called cotoletta alla milanese , a breaded meat cutlet traditionally made with veal. Centuries later, the recipe flew along in the minds of those looking for refugee in the Latinamerican lands becoming very popular in Argentina with the slight detail of using more commonly beef and chicken than veal. Argentinian version of the famous milanesa created in Buenos Aires in the 50s by Jose Napoli, owner and cook of a restaurant called Napoli.In an attempt to save some burnt milanesas he covered them with ham, cheese and tomato sauce. The recipe of course doesn't involve any burning.

Ingredients

A milanesa consists of a thin slice of meat (commonly beef, chicken, pork, or veal) that is coated in beaten egg, covered with breadcrumbs, and then or baked until
Below are the ingredients needed for this recipe (5 servings):

  • a little less than a finger slices of meat1kg
  • ham 200gr
  • mozzarella cheese 300gr
  • tomato sauce 1lts-2lts
  • 3 eggs
  • breadcrumbs or panko 1kg
  • salt
  • oregano
  • cumin
  • potato 1kg
  • one garlic clove
  • parsley

Steps

  1. Chop the garlic and parsley and them put it in a bowl with the eggs and the other condiments. add the meat, mix it and let it rest no more than 2 hours in the freezer.
  2. In the mean time, cut the potatoes into sticks and let them rest for a bit in a bowl with water. This will take out starch excess making them crunchier in the outside and softer in the inside.
  3. In another bowl, preferly flat and wide, put the breadcrumbs or panko. Also preheat your oven to 220°C and prepare an oiled up oven tray for your milanesas
  4. Now it is time for the coating. Follow this process, take a meat slice from the mixture bowl and put in the breadcrumb bowl. Roll the meat making sure it is well coated on both sides, you can apply pressure to make sure that those breadcrumbs stay sticked. Congrats you now have a raw milanesa!!!! put it in that oven tray. Repeat until you have no more meat to coat.
  5. Put the potato sticks in a tray, put both the potatoes and milanesas in the oven for 20 minutes.
  6. Once those 20 minutes have passed take the milanesas out of the oven. then try to equally distribute the ham, cheese and tomato sauce in this order among the milanesas.
  7. take them in the oven again and cook them for another 7 to 8 minutes.
  8. take all out of the oven and serve on a plate.
