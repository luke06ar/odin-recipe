Argentinian Asado

Description

The asado is one of the most traditional dishes in Argentina. It is more than just food, it a social event. Its origins go back to the gauchos, who used to cook meat slowly over fire in the open fields. Over time, this way of cooking became a tradition that is very important in Argentine culture. The key of a good asado is good quality meat and patience.

Ingredients

An asado consists of different cuts of meat cooked on a grill. Below are the ingredients needed for this recipe (5 servings):

  • short ribs (asado de tira) 1.5kg
  • chorizos 5 units
  • morcilla 2 units
  • vacío or flank steak 1kg
  • coarse salt (optional)
  • charcoal or firewood
  • chimichurri (optional)
  • bread

Steps

  1. Light the fire using charcoal or firewood and wait until you have hot embers. Do not cook with strong flames.
  2. Season the meat only with salt on both sides. (OPTIONAL)
  3. Place the meat on the grill with the bone or fat side down first.
  4. Cook the meat slowly and turn it only once to keep the juices inside.
  5. Add the chorizos and morcillas after the meat has been cooking for a while.
  6. Check the cooking point depending on your preference. Cooking time depends on each cut.
  7. Remove the meat from the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.
  8. Serve hot with bread and chimichurri.
